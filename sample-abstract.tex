\noindent
This research proposal addresses the challenge of lithology classification from well logs by leveraging self-supervised learning techniques to reduce dependence on expensive expert-labelled data. Traditional approaches require substantial manual labelling by domain experts, creating significant cost and time constraints for the oil and gas, mining and groundwater industries. The proposed study adapts the Value Imputation and Mask Estimation (VIME) framework to develop feature-aware self-supervised learning techniques specifically designed for spatiotemporal well log data. The research methodology follows a systematic four-experiment approach investigating domain-specific pretext tasks, feature-aware masking strategies, encoder architecture optimisation, and integrated framework validation. The experimental design utilises the FORCE 2020 and Volve Field datasets to ensure reproducible evaluation and cross-dataset generalisation assessment. Expected outcomes include quantitative comparisons with supervised baselines, label efficiency assessment, and cross-dataset generalisation evaluation. If successful, this research will contribute theoretical insights into representation learning for geological data and practical solutions for lithology classification in data-constrained environments, potentially reducing costs in subsurface characterisation across multiple industries.
